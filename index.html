The Process

How Elections Work

From announcement to results — every step explained simply.

📢
01

Election Announced

The Election Commission officially announces the election schedule, including dates for voting, model code of conduct, and deadlines.

📋
02

Voter Registration

Citizens verify or update their voter registration. New voters can enroll via Form 6. Rolls are published and corrections accepted.

🏛️
03

Candidate Nomination

Candidates file nomination papers, pay security deposits, and submit affidavits. Scrutiny and withdrawal windows follow.

📣
04

Campaign Period

Parties and candidates campaign, hold rallies, and broadcast manifesto pledges — all under the Model Code of Conduct.

🗳️
05

Polling Day

Voters visit booths with valid ID, biometrically verify identity, and cast votes on EVMs. Mock polls run in the morning.

📦
06

EVM Sealing & Storage

After voting, EVMs are sealed and stored in strong rooms under 24/7 CCTV surveillance until counting day.

🔢
07

Vote Counting

Counting agents from all parties watch as votes are tallied round by round. Results are announced per constituency.

🏆
08

Results & Oath

Winners receive certificates. Parties negotiate coalition if no majority. Elected representatives are sworn in by the President/Governor.

Key Dates

Election Timeline

A typical general election spans about 60–90 days from announcement to results.

Day 0

Election Announcement

Model Code of Conduct kicks in immediately. Election Commission announces full schedule.

Day 1 – 15

Voter List Finalisation

Last date to register or update voter details. Special Summary Revision completed.

Day 15 – 25

Nomination Window

Candidates file nominations, submit affidavits, and pay security deposits at Returning Officer offices.

Day 25 – 26

Scrutiny & Withdrawal

Nominations scrutinised for validity. Two-day withdrawal window for candidates to pull out.

Day 27 – 57

Campaign Period

Rallies, road shows, media campaigns. Campaign ends 48 hours before polling (silent period).

⭐
Day 60

Polling Day 🗳️

Voters cast ballots from 7 AM – 6 PM. Mock poll at 6 AM. VVPAT slips verify each vote.

Day 63 – 75

EVM Strong Room

Sealed EVMs stored under multi-layer security pending counting date set by ECI.

Day 75+

Counting & Results

Votes counted from 8 AM. Winners declared, certificates issued, government formation begins.

For Voters

Voter's Quick Guide

🪪

Valid ID at Polls

  • Voter ID Card (EPIC)
  • Aadhaar Card
  • Passport
  • Driving Licence
  • PAN Card
  • MNREGA Job Card
📝

How to Register

  • Visit voters.eci.gov.in
  • Fill Form 6 (new voters)
  • Upload documents online
  • Track application status
  • Receive e-EPIC card
🗳️

At the Polling Booth

  • Carry your Voter Slip + ID
  • Queue as per serial number
  • Biometric verification
  • Press EVM button for candidate
  • Verify VVPAT slip (7 seconds)
🚫

What NOT to Do

  • No mobile phones inside booth
  • No impersonation
  • No campaigning in 100m radius
  • No alcohol on polling day
  • Don't accept money/gifts
Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

You must be 18 years of age on or before the qualifying date (January 1st of the election year) to be eligible to vote. You also need to be a citizen of India and ordinarily resident in the constituency.

An Electronic Voting Machine (EVM) is a standalone device that records votes electronically. It is not connected to any network, making it hack-proof. VVPAT (Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail) machines print a paper slip that allows voters to verify their vote was recorded correctly.

The Model Code of Conduct (MCC) is a set of guidelines issued by the Election Commission to regulate the conduct of political parties and candidates during election campaigns. It comes into force immediately after the announcement of elections and remains in effect till results are declared. It prohibits misuse of government resources, hate speech, and bribery.

You must vote in the constituency where you are registered. However, the ECI allows remote voting for migrant workers under new pilot programs. You can also apply for an absentee ballot if you are a senior citizen (85+), disabled, or an essential services worker.

NOTA (None of the Above) is a ballot option that allows voters to formally reject all candidates on the ballot. It was introduced by the Supreme Court of India in 2013. If NOTA gets the highest votes, the candidate with the next highest votes wins (NOTA does not invalidate the election).

The Returning Officer of each constituency declares the result and issues a certificate of election to the winning candidate. The results are officially notified in the Gazette of India. For disputes, candidates can file an election petition in the High Court within 45 days.
